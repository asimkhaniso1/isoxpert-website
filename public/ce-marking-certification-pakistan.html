What is CE Marking?

A legal requirement to sell into the EU market.

CE Marking (Conformité Européenne) indicates a product meets EU health, safety and environmental protection requirements. It is mandatory for many product categories sold in the European Economic Area — including electrical and electronic equipment, machinery, toys, personal protective equipment (PPE) and medical devices. Without it, a covered product cannot legally be placed on the EU market, no matter how well it's made.

Unlike ISO management-system certification, CE Marking is a product conformity assessment against the specific EU directive(s) that apply — the process, documentation and any required testing depend on your product category.

For Pakistani manufacturers exporting to Europe, getting this right the first time avoids shipments being held at customs or rejected by buyers.

FAQ

CE Marking in Pakistan — common questions

What is CE Marking?

CE Marking is a conformity mark that indicates a product meets EU health, safety and environmental protection requirements. It is mandatory for many product categories sold within the European Economic Area, including electrical equipment, machinery, toys, PPE and medical devices.

Do Pakistani exporters need CE Marking?

Yes, if you manufacture and export products covered by CE directives to the EU or EEA. Without CE Marking, those products cannot legally be placed on the EU market, regardless of quality.

Can a Karachi-based body support CE Marking?

Yes. Company Certification International, headquartered in Karachi since 1997, guides Pakistani manufacturers through the applicable EU directives, technical documentation, testing coordination and Declaration of Conformity process needed to affix the CE mark.

How long does CE Marking take?

It depends on the product category and applicable directive — ranging from a few weeks for self-declaration categories to several months where third-party testing (Notified Body involvement) is required.
